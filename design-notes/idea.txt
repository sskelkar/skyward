I want to design a game for a team of software engineers that combines the gameplays of dungeons and dragons and a turn-based game like Rome: Total War. The premise of the game is that we are an airline startup. Each person will be assigned a 'director of engineering' role in different departments. For eg. director of ticket portal, director of advertising, director of recommendations, inventory etc. The point of this game is to teach these engineers to use claude agents to build systems that work, instead of implementing requirements by hand. So that they can adopt a manager mindset for working with agentic coding and also learn how to actually create claude agents and put them to work.

We can have scenarios like "we need to launch in one year". Each turn could be a quarter. So each director with the help of their org (a team of claude agents) will decide on some goals and deliver results. As the game progresses, the game runner can introduce randomness or scenarios like - "our stock is falling" or "we have a new competitor" or "feul prices are increasing" etc. The directors have to collaborate with each other, define strategy and deliver results in order to make this company successful. They are free to create dummy data in the appropriate format if required. There's no limit to how big an org they can grow. Their org is of course claude agents.

We can have the following roles assemble together to design the gameplay.
Dungeon & Dragons gamerunner: An experienced D&D gamerunner who knows how to design engaging and imaginative scenarios, character descriptions, situations etc. 
Dungeon & Dragons player: An avid D&D player who can give feedback on whether a new gameplay would be interesting from the point of view of players
Total War gamer: An avid Total War player with deep understanding of the turn-based gameplay of Total War. 
Claude Expert: Understands how Claude agents can be used and their limitations. The claude expert will help design realistic scenarios and tasks such that the Directors can actually accomplish them using agentic coding.
